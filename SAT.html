SAT Prep Course Curriculum

Our comprehensive two-week program covers all aspects of the SAT

Monday, Day 1 – Reading

Class Focus:

  • Overview of digital SAT format: structure, adaptive modules, on-screen tools.
  • General test-taking strategies: pacing (~1 min per Reading question), guess rather than omit.
  • Introduction to Reading section: short passages with one question each.
  • Core reading skills: identifying main ideas and key details.
  • Strategy: eliminate wrong answers, highlight key info, use "Mark for Review."
  • In-class passage and discussion.

Homework:

Complete Reading & Writing questions focused on reading comprehension. Use highlighting and note any tricky questions.

Tuesday, Day 2 – Writing

Class Focus:

  • Introduction to Writing section: grammar and sentence structure.
  • Focus on grammar rules: subject–verb agreement, pronouns, punctuation, run-ons/fragments.
  • Strategy: read full sentence, eliminate incorrect choices.
  • In-class grammar drills and discussion.

Homework:

Complete Writing questions focused on grammar rules. Review explanations for any errors.

Wednesday, Day 3 – Math (Algebra)

Class Focus:

  • Overview of digital SAT Math: structure, tools (graphing calculator, formula sheet).
  • Time management: ~1.5 min per question.
  • Focus on linear equations, inequalities, and systems.
  • Strategy: isolate variables, plug in answer choices, use calculator effectively.
  • In-class algebra problems.

Homework:

Complete algebra-focused Math questions (equations, inequalities, systems). Note questions to review in the next session.

Thursday, Day 4 – Reading (Inference & Vocab)

Class Focus:

  • Review homework from Day 1.
  • Focus on making inferences and understanding vocabulary in context.
  • Strategy: context clues, eliminate obviously wrong answers, flag tough questions.
  • Timed mini drill and discussion.

Homework:

Complete Reading questions focused on inference and vocab. Highlight evidence and bring questions to discuss.

Friday, Day 5 – Math (Problem Solving & Data)

Class Focus:

  • Review algebra homework from Day 3.
  • Focus on ratios, percentages, interpreting charts and graphs, basic stats.
  • Strategy: translate word problems step-by-step, estimate before solving.
  • In-class data analysis problems.

Homework:

Complete Math questions focused on data and problem solving. Mark any questions that took too long.

Monday, Day 6 – Writing (Style & Organization)

Class Focus:

  • Review grammar homework from Day 2.
  • Focus on transitions, sentence order, redundancy, conciseness.
  • Strategy: identify purpose of sentences, choose transitions that fit logic.
  • In-class editing of a short passage.

Homework:

Complete Writing questions on transitions, conciseness, and organization. Note rules involved in wrong answers.

Tuesday, Day 7 – Math (Advanced)

Class Focus:

  • Review Day 5 homework.
  • Focus on quadratics, exponentials, and functions.
  • Strategy: break into small steps, use graphing tools to check work.
  • In-class problems on quadratics and function graphs.

Homework:

Complete Math questions on advanced algebra (quadratics, exponentials, functions). Note where you got stuck.

Wednesday, Day 8 – Reading (Mixed Practice)

Class Focus:

  • Review inference and vocab homework.
  • Mixed passage set: literature, science, and historical.
  • Strategy: apply all reading strategies under timed conditions.
  • In-class mini section and review.

Homework:

Complete one full Reading & Writing module (official source if possible) under timed conditions.

Thursday, Day 9 – Writing (Mixed Practice)

Class Focus:

  • Review style homework.
  • Practice combining grammar and style questions.
  • Cover final topics: parallel structure, misplaced modifiers, idioms.
  • Strategy: quickly determine grammar vs. style question.
  • In-class quiz and review.

Homework:

Complete mixed Writing questions. Apply full strategy approach and review missed answers carefully.

Friday, Day 10 – Math (Geometry & Final Review)

Class Focus:

  • Review advanced math homework.
  • Focus on geometry formulas, coordinate geometry, basic trig.
  • Strategy: draw diagrams, use formula sheet, estimate.
  • Final review of digital test tools and pacing strategies.
  • In-class geometry/trig practice and final Q&A.

Homework:

No new homework. Review all prior drills, notes, and strategies. Prepare for test day with confidence.
